## Design Context

### Users
The primary audience is academic peers, hiring and search committees, research collaborators, and readers who arrive through publications, CV links, or project pages. They need to quickly understand Ming Ma's research identity, institutional credibility, current projects, publications, and how to make contact.

### Brand Personality
Professional, restrained, and intellectually precise. The site should feel calm, confident, research-driven, and memorable without becoming decorative or promotional. It should communicate a scholar working at the intersection of AI governance, public service transformation, computational social science, comparative politics, and power/knowledge distribution.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use an English-first, light, spacious "minimal research atlas" direction. Favor warm off-white surfaces, ink-like typography, subtle teal/blue and amber accents, fine-line maps, institutional diagrams, and a few locally generated illustration stickers. Avoid heavy 3D dependencies, glassmorphism, gradient blobs, generic icon grids, dark neon AI aesthetics, and visual clutter.

### Design Principles
- Lead with research identity: name, role, institution, ERC/BiASE-AI project, research themes, and direct actions should be visible immediately.
- Keep content maintainable: publication, update, and project information should continue to live in the structured content source.
- Make the research theme visible: use lightweight animated networks, maps, annotations, and sticker-like illustrations to suggest AI, administration, public service, institutions, and knowledge distribution.
- Design for academic scanning: typography, section rhythm, and metadata should help visitors evaluate credibility quickly.
- Respect accessibility and performance: local assets, readable contrast, responsive layouts, keyboard-friendly links, and reduced-motion support are required.
